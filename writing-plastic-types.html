Plastic Type Categorisation

15 June 2023 Plastic · Recycling · Education

There is no one in this modern world that has never used plastic. Your clothes, vehicle, household appliances, and even the device you’re reading this on is made from plastics. Plastic has helped human life a lot, but because of the irresponsible way we handle plastic waste, it eventually became a disaster.

There is a great extent of plastic types — some of them can be recycled, and some of them can’t (or are not worth recycling). I want to share a bit about plastic types and clear up a common misconception.

What Do the Numbers Mean?

You’ve probably seen the symbol on the plastic products you use, at least once. The number is called the RIC (Resin Identification Code) — a set of symbols appearing on plastic products that identify the plastic resin out of which the product is made. The numbers vary from 1 to 7.

Most people think the numbers symbolise how many times the plastic can be used. The truth is, the number identifies the material that makes the product.

Many of my friends think a plastic bottle (number 1) can’t be used again after one use, and a shampoo bottle (number 2) can be reused because it has a higher number. But that’s not what the codes mean at all.

The Seven Plastic Types

01

PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) — Clear bottles, food packaging. Fully recyclable.

02

HDPE (High Density Polyethylene) — Milk jugs, detergent bottles. Easy to recycle.

03

PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) — Pipes, window frames. Difficult and toxic to recycle.

04

LDPE (Low Density Polyethylene) — Plastic bags, squeeze bottles. Recyclable at some facilities.

05

PP (Polypropylene) — Yogurt containers, bottle caps. Increasingly recyclable.

06

PS (Polystyrene) — Styrofoam, disposable cutlery. Rarely recycled.

07

Other — Mixed or specialty plastics. Generally not recyclable.

Understanding these codes is the first step toward smarter waste sorting. Next time you see a number stamped on a plastic product, you’ll know exactly what it means — and whether it belongs in the recycling bin.
