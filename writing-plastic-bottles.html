Plastic Bottle Fun Facts

20 July 2023 Plastic · Recycling · Environment

Most plastic bottles are formed by three kinds of different plastic types — PET, HDPE, and PVC. Each serves a different purpose and carries a different environmental footprint. Here’s a quick breakdown of what makes up the bottle in your hand.

The Three Plastics in Every Bottle

01

PET — The transparent bottle. PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) is a clear, strong, and lightweight plastic widely used for packaging foods and beverages. It is fully recyclable — it can be commercially recycled by thorough washing and re-melting, or by chemically breaking it down to its component materials. Identified by the #1 symbol in the Plastic Code.

02

HDPE — The bottle cap. HDPE (High Density Polyethylene) is a very durable, robust, and commonly used plastic that is relatively easy to recycle. Found in both rigid and soft plastic forms — from milk containers and shampoo bottles to detergent bottles and kid’s toys. Identified by the #2 symbol.

03

PVC — The plastic label. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) can be easily welded to other plastics and has a lower cost than other types. Unfortunately, PVC is a well-established carcinogen most strongly associated with liver cancer. It is the most environmentally damaging plastic — its lifecycle results in the release of toxic, chlorine-based chemicals building up in water, air, and the food chain.

PVC is the only material used for plastic bottles which is toxic and dangerous for our health and environment. The burning of PVC is responsible for 80% of dioxin air pollution.

Next time you pick up a plastic bottle, take a closer look at the numbers stamped on each part. Understanding what your bottle is made of is the first step toward making better choices for recycling and for the environment.
